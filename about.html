How it started

The spreadsheet that became an app.

In 2023, trying to figure out whether the Queensland Shared Equity Scheme actually made sense for our budget, we built a spreadsheet. It had 14 tabs, nested formulas, and still couldn't tell us what we owed the government in year 10.

We started talking to other first home buyers and heard the same story over and over. The scheme is genuinely useful ‚Äî it lets people who couldn't otherwise afford a deposit get into the market. But it's complex, and the complexity was stopping people from using it confidently.

"We wanted a tool that told you the full cost, the 30-year picture, and the break-even point ‚Äî all from one place."

So we built mypropertymate. It started as an internal tool, went through dozens of iterations shaped by feedback from real buyers, and is now available to anyone navigating the path to homeownership in Australia.

We're a small team ‚Äî we answer support tickets ourselves, we ship updates based on real feature requests, and we care deeply about making this genuinely useful. Not startup-speak useful. Actually useful.
